THE QUIET PATH

MacBook Pro.
The elegant choice.

Unified memory changes everything. No PCIe copies. No VRAM limits that feel arbitrary. The entire pool is available to the GPU at full bandwidth. MLX and llama.cpp Metal turn a 64 GB or 128 GB M4/M5 Max into a shockingly competent local inference machine — and it does it in silence.

This is the option for people who want serious local models without dedicating a room or accepting fan noise. You can run a 70 B Q4 at respectable speed on a couch, in a café, or on a plane.

THE SOFTWARE LAYER
Two tools turned consumer GPUs into personal AI infrastructure.
llama.cpp
The great equalizer
Runs on every piece of hardware on this page. The 3060, the 8× 3090 rig, the DGX Spark, and the MacBook Pro all speak GGUF. Quantization is the reason a 24 GB card can run a 70 B model at all. This single project turned the used GPU market into the backbone of the local AI movement.
GGUF • Metal • CUDA • Vulkan • CPU fallback • The reason the 3090 community exists
vLLM
The throughput engine
When you have the memory (3090 multi-GPU, 6000 Pro, DGX Spark) and you need a real local inference server with paged attention and continuous batching, vLLM is the reason it feels production-grade. OpenAI-compatible endpoints on your own hardware, at your own cost, with your own data.
PagedAttention • Continuous batching • 10–50× higher throughput than naive serving on the same hardware
